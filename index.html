About

I am now a Ph.D. student in Dr. Matthew Lew's lab at Washington University in St. Louis. My current research focuses on Single-Molecule Orientation Localization Microscopy (SMOLM) and Point Spread Function (PSF) engineering.

I received my Bachelor's degree at Nankai University. During that time, I was a member of the Physics Poling Class. My undergraduate research was advised by Dr. Leiting Pan, focusing on applying different biophysical technologies on human Red Blood Cells (hRBCs), including exploring the morphology and deformability of hRBC using microfluidic chip, and revealing the structure of hRBC membrane cytoskeleton using super-resolution microscopy (specifically, STochastic Optical Reconstruction Microscopy, STORM). I have also worked as a research intern at Dr. Qiong Yang's lab at Department of Biophysics, University of Michigan, during the summer of 2019.

Research Experiences

  • Human Heart Organoid Morphology Characterization by Optical Coherence Tomography
    Department of Biomedical Engineering, Washington University in St. Louis, 2022.1~2022.5, Advisor: Dr. Chao Zhou

    • Maintained human induced pluripotent stem cells (hiPSCs) and cultured self-assembled human heart organoids (hHOs)
    • Analyzed longitudinal changes of the hHO morphology and structure by imaging with Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT)
    • Characterized different cell types in mature hHOs by multi-color immunofluorescent labeling and two-photon imaging

  • Study of Human Red Blood Cells Based on Microfluidics and Super-resolution Microscopy
    School of Physics, Nankai University, 2019.4~2021.6, Advisor: Dr. Leiting Pan

    • Mimicked the capillary environment in vitro by microfluidic chip and explored the deformation process of hRBCs when traveling through narrow microfluidic channels
    • Designed microfluidic ratchet chip for sorting the hRBCs in different life stages
    • Imaged the structure of actin-spectrin network on hRBC membrane cytoskeleton and hRBC membrane protein CD47 diffusivity using STORM, together with related data analysis using MATLAB

  • Immunofluorescent Biomarker for Zebrafish Somitogenesis and Single Cell Oscillation
    Department of Biophysics, University of Michigan, 2019.7~2019.9, Advisor: Dr. Qiong Yang

    • Developed immunofluorescent biomarkers for different signaling pathway in zebrafish segementation clock
    • Distinguished oscillating cells in different phenotypes during the somitogenesis process
    Results about this research is published as a poster on APS March Meeting 2020
